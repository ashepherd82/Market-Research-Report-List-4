<p><p>Biomass steam turbines can be categorized into three main types based on their operation: condensing, back pressure, and others. Condensing turbines are primarily used in power generation and are designed to maximize energy production efficiency by condensing the steam after use. Back pressure turbines, on the other hand, are commonly used in industrial processes to meet both electricity and thermal energy requirements. The "Others" category includes special-purpose turbines designed for specific applications outside of conventional power generation and industrial processes.</p></p>
