<p><p>Dairy fermentation starters are categorized into three main types: mesophilic, thermophilic, and probiotics. Mesophilic starters work best at moderate temperatures, while thermophilic starters thrive in high temperatures. Probiotics contain live beneficial bacteria that promote gut health and digestion. Mesophilic starters are commonly used in making cheeses like cheddar and brie, while thermophilic starters are used in yogurts and some Italian cheeses. Probiotics, on the other hand, are added to dairy products to enhance their nutritional value and offer health benefits to consumers.</p></p>
