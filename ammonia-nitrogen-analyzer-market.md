<p><ul><li>Colorimetric Method</li><li>Electrode Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Ammonia Nitrogen Analyzers can be classified into two main types - Colorimetric Method and Electrode Method. Colorimetric Method analyzers work by measuring the color change when ammonia reacts with a reagent, providing a visual indication of ammonia levels. On the other hand, Electrode Method analyzers use an electrode to detect ammonia ions in a solution, providing accurate and real-time measurements. Both types of analyzers are commonly used in various industries for monitoring ammonia levels in water and wastewater treatment processes.</p></p>
