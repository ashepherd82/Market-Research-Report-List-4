<p><p>Food phosphates are essential additives used in the food industry for various functions such as emulsification, moisture retention, leavening, and flavor enhancement. The different types of food phosphates commonly used include Sodium Tripolyphosphate (STPP), Sodium Hexametaphosphate (SHMP), Sodium Acid Pyrophosphate (SAPP), and Tetrasodium Pyrophosphate (TSPP). STPP is typically used for seafood and meat processing, SHMP for cheese and dairy products, SAPP for bakery goods, and TSPP for canned fruits and vegetables. Each type of phosphate serves a specific purpose in enhancing the quality and shelf-life of various food products.</p></p>
